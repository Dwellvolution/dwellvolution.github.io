LIQUID SOIL APPLIED TERMITICIDE

HOW DOES A LIQUID SOIL TERMITICIDE APPLIED?

A trench approximately _____ wide and deep is digged around the perimeter of the building. The trench is injected with non repellent chemical liquid termiticide that binds with the soil.

As termites forage through the soil, they come into contact with the non repellent liquid termiticide and passes the chemical to othe termites. As termites cannot detect the chemical, the entire colony will be eliminated over the time. The active ingredient has a growth inhibitor that when it takes over, it prevents termite from maturing through molting, then they die off. This is what makes this treatment method effective.

Non-repellent chemical liquid termiticide have an environmental profile that is safe for those with pets and children yet effective in managing termite infestation.

However, as a precaution, we recommend pets and children kept away from the treatment in around ______ hours to dry the termiticide.

If live termites or infestations are located outside or inside the building, an immediate treatment is performed, and a follow up inspection to ensure that infestation is managed or before possible termite damage occurs.

COST OF CHEMICAL LIQUID TERMITE TREATMENT

Cost for chemical termite management depends on the size of the property being treated. Cost is calculated per lineal meter.

HOW LONG DOES THE APPLICATION OF LIQUID CHEMICAL TAKES PLACE?

The application of liquid chemical termiticide, will depend on the size of the property being treated.

However, non-repellent chemical liquid termiticide begins working once termites ingest it. The faster worker termites gets in contact with the termiticide, the faster their colony will be eliminated. Colony elimination is the most effective way to protect your property.
